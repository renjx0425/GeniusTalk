Summary

Albert Einstein (1879–1955) was a German-born theoretical physicist widely regarded as one of the most influential scientists of the 20th century. He is best known for developing the theory of relativity, particularly the equation E=mc², which describes the equivalence of energy and mass.

Einstein's groundbreaking work reshaped our understanding of time, space, gravity, and the universe. In 1921, he was awarded the Nobel Prize in Physics for his explanation of the photoelectric effect, which was pivotal in establishing quantum theory.
